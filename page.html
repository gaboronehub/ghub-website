Who are we?

The Young AI Leaders Community is grounded in the belief that the future of AI depends not just on the progress of technology but on the values and vision of those who shape it. At the Gaborone Hub, we are committed to empowering emerging talent with the knowledge, platforms, and support needed to champion ethical, inclusive, and socially impactful AI.

Our focus is on cultivating a collaborative and diverse environment where innovation thrives. With access to mentorship, resources, and global networks, young leaders in our community are equipped to turn bold ideas into transformative solutions. As part of the global AI for Good movement, the Gaborone Hub is creating a space where youth-led initiatives can thrive, driving meaningful change and helping address Botswana’s and Africa’s most pressing challenges through responsible AI.

The Young AI Leaders community is an initiative started by AI for Good. AI for Good is organized by ITU in partnership with over 40 UN Sister Agencies and co-convened with the Government of Switzerland. AI for Good is identifying trustworthy AI applications, building skills and standards, and advancing AI governance for sustainable development.

Disclaimer: The views and opinions expressed by Young AI Leaders are solely their own and do not represent the official views of the International Telecommunication Union.

Membership

Each Young AI Leaders Hub, including the Gaborone Hub, is intentionally limited to a maximum of 50 members. This structure fosters a tight-knit, collaborative atmosphere where every member can engage meaningfully and contribute. After completing their four-year journey within the hub, members transition into alumni status, allowing them to remain connected to the community and continue supporting its vision of responsible and impactful AI leadership.

Why Join the Gaborone Hub of Young AI Leaders?

  • Access to an international platform that empowers young changemakers to develop and showcase AI-driven solutions for real-world challenges, with support from industry experts, global institutions, and the broader AI for Good ecosystem.
  • Opportunities to build relationships with experienced mentors, thought leaders, and peers across continents, while benefiting from training programs that sharpen your skills and elevate your presence in the AI space.
  • The chance to collaborate on both regional and global initiatives aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), with visibility at international events and conferences.
  • A space to lead impactful projects rooted in ethical AI, diversity, and inclusivity, directly addressing the needs of your local community.
  • The ability to engage with a vibrant network through hybrid events and initiatives, bridging virtual collaboration with in-person innovation and collective impact.

Your Commitment as a Member

Joining the Gaborone Hub is both a privilege and a responsibility. As a selected member of the Young AI Leaders community, you agree to uphold the following commitments:

  • Support and align yourself with the mission and objectives outlined in our Community Charter.
  • Demonstrate the values and conduct expected within our global community, in accordance with the Code of Conduct.
  • Attend a minimum of 8 out of the 12 scheduled hub meetings annually, which can be either in person or online.
  • Contribute meaningfully by participating in at least one Gaborone Hub project each year, as well as regional projects with other hubs.
